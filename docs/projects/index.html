Explore with Shiny: Creating a Statistics Inquiry-Based Learning Tool

Creating a dynamic interactive shiny application in R to better students’ understanding of statistics and data science. The application focuses on bootstrapping and permutations. The concepts of bootstrapping and permutations are staples in undergraduate statistics and data science courses, but are often difficult to understand conceptually and perform in practice. Our application provides students the opportunity to learn about these concepts through investigation, while simultaneously developing the needed coding skills to perform these concepts on their own. Currently, their exists interactive web applications as means for students to learn about bootstrapping and permutations. However, they solely focus on conceptualizing bootstrapping and permutations, leaving students without the necessary skills to implement such concepts in practice.

This project aims to support teaching at Duke and other universities by continuing the advancement of statistics and data science education, specifically at the undergraduate level. Further, this application sets the foundation for future work to best understand how students learn to code and implement these processes when first being introduced to bootstrapping and permutations.

This project plans to extend to include the development of assessment questions surrounding the application.
